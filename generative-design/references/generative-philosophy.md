# Generative Algorithmic Philosophies

Named computational aesthetic movements for generative design work.
Each is a system that produces infinite unique outputs within a defined aesthetic.

---

## Organic Turbulence
**Movement name**: "Organic Turbulence"
**Core tension**: Chaos constrained by natural law.
**Algorithm**: Multi-octave Perlin noise flow fields. Thousands of particles follow
vector forces, trails accumulating into organic density maps. Fast particles burn bright;
slow ones fade to near-transparency. The system runs to equilibrium — a meticulously
tuned balance where stillness and motion coexist.
**Visual signature**: Dark backgrounds. Long, flowing particle trails. Dense in the center,
sparse at the edges. Color mapped to velocity — warm for fast, cool for slow.
**Parameters to expose**: Particle count, noise scale, trail length, color temperature range.

## Crystalline Order
**Movement name**: "Crystalline Order"
**Core tension**: Perfect structure emerging from local rules.
**Algorithm**: Voronoi tessellation with Lloyd's relaxation. Seeds placed via Poisson
disk sampling for even distribution. Cell borders rendered at varying weights by cell
size. Interior filled with a noise-displaced gradient unique to each cell.
**Visual signature**: Faceted, gem-like. Each cell is a distinct world. The overall
composition reads as a single surface; up close, infinite variation.
**Parameters to expose**: Seed count, relaxation iterations, border weight, color palette.

---

## Emergent Stillness
**Movement name**: "Emergent Stillness"
**Core tension**: Complexity that resolves into apparent simplicity.
**Algorithm**: Reaction-diffusion (Gray-Scott model). Two chemical species interact
across a grid, producing spots, stripes, labyrinthine forms, and spirals depending
on feed rate and kill rate parameters. The system runs for thousands of iterations
before the pattern stabilizes.
**Visual signature**: Biological, cellular, almost anatomical. Reminiscent of coral,
leopard skin, brain tissue. Monochrome or very limited palette — color would distract
from the form.
**Parameters to expose**: Feed rate (F), kill rate (k), iterations, resolution.

## Phyllotactic Fields
**Movement name**: "Phyllotactic Fields"
**Core tension**: Nature's mathematics made visible.
**Algorithm**: Points placed at the golden angle (137.508°) around a growing spiral,
mimicking the arrangement of seeds in a sunflower. Each point is a small mark — a dot,
a line, a petal shape — scaled by its distance from the center. Color cycles through
a hue range synchronized to the growth sequence.
**Visual signature**: Spiraling, centrifugal, almost hypnotic. Mathematical yet organic.
The golden ratio is the hidden structure. Works at any scale.
**Parameters to expose**: Point count, mark shape, size curve, color range, rotation speed.

---

## Quantum Harmonics
**Movement name**: "Quantum Harmonics"
**Core tension**: Invisible forces made tangible.
**Algorithm**: Interference patterns generated by multiple sine wave sources at
irrational frequency ratios. The superposition of waves creates Moiré-like regions
of constructive and destructive interference. Color maps to wave amplitude.
**Visual signature**: Rippling, luminous. Bright nodes where waves reinforce, dark
voids where they cancel. The pattern is never static — it evolves slowly as
phase relationships drift.
**Parameters to expose**: Wave sources (count), frequencies, amplitudes, color map, animation speed.

---

## Urban Strata
**Movement name**: "Urban Strata"
**Core tension**: Human order imposed on natural entropy.
**Algorithm**: Subdivision algorithm (similar to Mondrian generation) creates a
hierarchical grid. Each cell is then noise-displaced at its edges, breaking the
perfect geometry with organic irregularity. Cells are filled with textures generated
from layered simplex noise at different scales.
**Visual signature**: Architectural, structural. Reads like an aerial map or a cross-section
of geological layers. The tension between the grid and the noise is the subject.
**Parameters to expose**: Initial grid divisions, noise displacement amount, texture scale,
palette (geological vs. urban vs. biological).

## Writing a New Algorithmic Philosophy

1. **Name it** (1–2 words): Captures the core tension, sounds like an art movement
2. **Core tension**: What two opposing forces does the algorithm embody?
3. **Algorithm** (2–3 sentences): What mathematical process drives it?
4. **Visual signature**: What does a piece in this movement unmistakably look like?
5. **Parameters**: What 3–4 values should the user be able to adjust?
